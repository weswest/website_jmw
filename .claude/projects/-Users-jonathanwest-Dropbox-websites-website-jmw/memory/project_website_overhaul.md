Website overhaul initiated 2026-03-15.

**Positioning decisions (locked 2026-03-15):**
- Board/advisory as hero frame, but site must also serve AI Analytics role search
- Actively looking for next role but CANNOT be publicly positioned as job-seeking. No "hire me" CTAs. Low-key "here's how I think and what I do" — let the work speak.
- Current employer (Nomis) in final days of PE ownership; financial outcome disappointing.
- Patent: CANNOT be mentioned anywhere, on advice of counsel. But the *work* around AI agents, human-in-the-loop workflows, structured domain reasoning is fair game. Dance around the knowledge graph without describing it specifically.

**Core differentiators (Wes's own framing):**
1. Broadly-versed in analytics → quickly identifies *how* to solve complex problems
2. Exceptional at accuracy/performance vs. complexity tradeoffs → right tool for the right problem ("the complexity tax")
3. Communicates technical solutions to non-technical C-suite audiences
- "Leading from the front" — always hands-dirty, builds phenomenal teams through that process

**Content decisions:**
- Hosting Nomis articles: full text on site, link back to Nomis original
- Hosting Novantas articles: full text on site (originals no longer online)
- Blog: largely professional AI think pieces, maybe some recipes. NOT personal.
- Stay Positive (creative nonfiction): absolutely NOT on the site
- Management book pipeline: secondary, may appear in blog eventually
- Substack: distribution layer, not canonical home (per Perplexity research)

**How to apply:** Every design decision should pass the test: "Would a CEO scanning this for 60 seconds see someone worth a conversation?" without it feeling like a job application.
